Background

Nasarawa state is located in the North Central region of Nigeria. It is bordered to the east by the states of Taraba and Plateau, to the north by Kaduna State, to the south by the states of Kogi and Benue, and to the west by the Federal Capital Territory. The state was formed from the west of Plateau State on 1 October 1996.

Population

Of the 36 states of Nigeria, Nasarawa is the fifteenth largest in area and second least populous with an estimated population of about 2.5 million as of 2016.
